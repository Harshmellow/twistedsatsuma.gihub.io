Early Childhood

My passion for technology has been with me from the beggining of my brains consinanace, I remember being 5 in El salvador and my mom giving me a atari type video game machine with all sorts of games built into it, at the time I couldnt stop playing Super Mario Bros which was the old game that kids in the 80's played hours upon hours but for me even in El salvador its what kept me out of boredom of such a vile and destructive place San salvador is. Aside from that gaming system the only other things I can remeber from El salvador are either gruesome or just superficial and not important in depicting my early childhood. As many who know me personally from school, I tend to have an attitude towards authority figures and this is not the way is was born.



Life in El Salvador

While there were things that made me happy in El salvador like the taste of a pupusa there aren't many things that I can remember that would be considered positive to many. School in El salvador was a luxury that many kids did not get the chance to have, --atleast a safe and directed education that was--, this meant that my mom had to work day and night to provide such a luxury to me.



Hearing stories of how my mom got to work everyday walking miles everyday, having to wake hours earlier than most of the world, gave me perspective of what it takes to be succesful in this world and make the plata. This helped me and I hope it can help others get the motivation to do things that they thought they couldnt do. Even if that means going past what societys labels you as. Thinking that I would live in America speaking english, going to a geat public school just is surreal to my el savadorain self. It didnt take much to make that part of my self happy, infact all it took was a joystick and a box T.V. But im glad that I didnt raised by some rich snob who stompts on others to preserve there way of life.



Damn right I like the life I live because I went from negative to positive.
